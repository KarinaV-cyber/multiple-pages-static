A crescent-shaped bun that you always desire. The modern croissant was invented by French chef Sylvain Claudius Goy in 1915, though its origins can be traced back to the Austrian pastry known as the kipferl. August Zang, an Austrian artillery officer, is credited with introducing the kipferl to Paris in 1839, which Parisian bakers later adapted into the croissant we know today.
